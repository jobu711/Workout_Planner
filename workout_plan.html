Shoulder Exercises

Pre-Workout Warm-Up Every Session

Complete this 5-minute warm-up sequence before every session to protect your shoulders and improve performance.

Band Pull-Aparts

Hold band at chest height, arms extended. Pull band apart by squeezing shoulder blades. Control the return.

2 sets x 15-20 reps Light band

Targets: Rear Deltoid, Rhomboids, Mid Traps

Face Pulls (Light)

Cable at face height with rope. Pull toward forehead, externally rotate at end (hands beside ears). Squeeze 2 seconds.

2 sets x 15 reps Light weight

Targets: Rear Deltoid, Rotator Cuff, Mid Traps

Key: External rotation at the end is critical

Cable/Band External Rotation at 0deg

Stand sideways to cable/band anchor at elbow height. Elbow pinned to side, rotate forearm away from body.

1 set x 15 reps each Light band

Targets: Infraspinatus, Teres Minor

Wall Angels

Stand with back flat against wall, arms in goalpost position. Slowly slide arms up overhead while keeping contact with wall, then back down.

2 sets x 10 reps Bodyweight

Targets: Shoulder Mobility, Thoracic Spine, Posture

Shoulder Pass-Throughs

Hold band or towel with wide grip overhead. Keeping arms straight, rotate shoulders to bring band behind your back, then return.

2 sets x 10 reps Band/Towel

Targets: Shoulder Flexibility, Chest, Lats

Tip: Use wider grip if mobility is limited

Pressing Movements Compound

Overhead pressing movements build overall shoulder mass and strength.

3 sets x 8-12 reps

Stand with barbell at shoulder height. Press overhead while engaging core for stability. Lower with control.

3 sets x 6-10 reps

Dumbbell Lateral Raise

Hold dumbbells at sides. Lift arms out to sides until shoulder height, keeping slight bend in elbows. Lower with control.

3 sets x 12-15 reps

Targets: Side Deltoid, Trapezius, Supraspinatus

Cable Lateral Raise

Stand beside cable machine. Lift arm outward to side with slight elbow bend. Cable provides constant tension throughout.

3 sets x 12-15 reps

Targets: Side Deltoid, Trapezius, Supraspinatus

Lever Lateral Raise

Sit on machine and raise arms out to sides against resistance. Machine guides movement for proper form.

3 sets x 12-15 reps

Targets: Side Deltoid, Trapezius, Rotator Cuff

Dumbbell Front Raise

Hold dumbbells in front of thighs. Lift one or both arms forward to shoulder height. Lower with control.

3 sets x 12-15 reps

Targets: Anterior Deltoid, Lateral Deltoid, Supraspinatus

Kettlebell Single Arm Thruster

Hold kettlebell at shoulder height, descend into a squat until thighs reach parallel, then drive upward while pressing overhead in one fluid motion.

3 sets x 8-10 reps each side

Targets: Shoulders, Quads, Glutes, Triceps, Core

Upright Rows Compound

Upright rows target shoulders and upper traps. Keep elbows above wrists.

Hold dumbbells in front. Pull up toward chin with elbows leading. Keep weights close to body.

3 sets x 10-12 reps

Targets: Trapezius, Deltoids, Biceps

3 sets x 10-12 reps

Targets: Deltoids, Trapezius, Biceps

3 sets x 10-12 reps

Kettlebell Training - By Muscle Group

Warm-Up Essential

After shoulder prep, complete these kettlebell-specific warm-ups to prepare for the main workout.

Slingshot Warm-Up

Pass the kettlebell around your body at waist height, switching hands in front and behind. Keep your core tight and hips stable.

20 reps each direction

Targets: Shoulders, Core, Grip

Good Morning Warm-Up

Hold KB at chest or behind head. Hinge at hips, pushing butt back while keeping back flat. Feel the stretch in hamstrings.

10 reps

Targets: Glutes, Hamstrings

Figure 8 Warm-Up

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Pass the KB between your legs in a figure-8 pattern, switching hands as it passes through. Keep core engaged and movement fluid.

15 reps each direction

Targets: Core, Obliques, Shoulders, Grip, Glutes

Wrist Curl Warm-Up

Sit on bench with elbow resting on thigh, palm facing up. Hold KB and slowly lower it toward ground, then curl back up using only wrist. Repeat both sides.

2 sets x 12-15 reps each

Targets: Forearm Flexors, Wrist, Grip

Tip: Helps prevent wrist injuries and improves grip strength

Posterior Chain Glutes, Hamstrings, Lower Back

Hip-hinge dominant movements that build explosive power and posterior strength. Master the hip hinge pattern for all kettlebell work.

Two Handed Swing

The foundational KB movement. Hinge at hips, swing KB between legs, then explosively drive hips forward. Arms stay relaxed.

3 sets x 15-20 reps Watch

Targets: Glutes, Hamstrings, Lower Back, Core

Goal: 10 minutes non-stop

Pirate Ships

Hold KB in one hand and swing it between legs while simultaneously lifting opposite leg off ground and extending it behind. Alternate sides in fluid motion.

3 sets x 10-12 reps each side

Targets: Glutes, Hamstrings, Core, Balance

Tip: Keep non-stepping leg straight for better balance and engagement

Legs Quads & Glutes

Squat and lunge variations to build lower body strength, stability, and mobility.

Kettlebell Core Training Core Focus

Rotational and anti-rotational movements that build functional core strength and stability with kettlebells.

Kneeling Around the Body

Kneel on both knees with kettlebell in front. Pass the kettlebell around your body at waist level, switching hands behind your back and in front. Keep core tight and avoid rotating torso.

3 sets x 10 each direction Video

Targets: Core, Obliques, Anti-Rotation

Perform a kettlebell swing but rotate your torso at the top of the movement, directing the bell to one side. Alternate sides with each rep while maintaining hip hinge mechanics.

3 sets x 10-12 each side Video

Targets: Core, Obliques, Glutes, Hamstrings

Rotational Shoulder Toss

Hold kettlebell at chest level. Rotate explosively through the core and toss the bell from one hand to the other across your body. Control the catch and repeat in opposite direction.

3 sets x 8-10 each side Video

Targets: Core, Obliques, Shoulders, Rotational Power

Side to Side Leg Raises

Lie on your back holding kettlebell overhead with straight arms. Raise legs together and lower them to one side, then back up and to the other side. Keep lower back pressed to floor.

3 sets x 8-10 each side Video

Targets: Obliques, Lower Abs, Hip Flexors

Hamstring Exercises Home-Friendly

Build strong hamstrings with exercises you can do at home with minimal equipment.

Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift

Stand with dumbbells in front of thighs. Hinge at hips, keeping back straight and knees slightly bent. Lower weights along legs until you feel hamstring stretch, then drive hips forward to return.

3-4 sets x 8-12 reps

Single Leg Dumbbell Deadlift

Stand on one leg holding dumbbell(s). Hinge at hip, extending free leg back for balance. Lower weight while keeping back straight. Great for balance and unilateral strength.

3 sets x 8-10 each leg

Glute Bridge

Lie on back, feet flat on floor near glutes. Drive through heels to lift hips up, squeezing glutes at top. Lower with control. Add weight on hips for progression.

Quadriceps Exercises Home-Friendly

Build strong quads with bodyweight and dumbbell exercises.

Bodyweight Squat

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Lower by bending knees and hips, keeping chest up. Descend until thighs are parallel or below, then drive through heels to stand.

3-4 sets x 15-20 reps

Targets: Quads, Glutes, Core

Dumbbell Lunge

Hold dumbbells at sides. Step forward with one leg, lowering back knee toward floor. Front thigh should reach parallel. Push through front heel to return to start.

3 sets x 10-12 each leg

Targets: Quads, Glutes, Hamstrings

Dumbbell Split Squat

Hold dumbbells, stand in split stance. Lower back knee toward floor while keeping torso upright. Press through front foot to return. Don't alternate - complete all reps on one side first.

3 sets x 10-12 each leg

Targets: Quads, Glutes

Bulgarian Split Squat

Hold dumbbells, place rear foot elevated on bench or chair. Lower into single leg squat position. Excellent quad and glute builder with bonus balance training.

3 sets x 8-12 each leg

Targets: Quads, Glutes, Balance

Dumbbell Step Up

Hold dumbbells at sides. Step up onto bench or sturdy platform with one foot. Drive through heel to lift body up. Step down with control. Functional leg strength.

3 sets x 10-12 each leg

Targets: Quads, Glutes, Balance

Lever Leg Extension

Sit with back against pad, feet under lever arm. Grasp side handles for stability. Extend legs until fully straight, hold briefly, then lower with control. Isolates the quadriceps.

3-4 sets x 10-15 reps

Targets: Quadriceps

Tip: Machine isolates quads and may help improve knee stability. Adjust seat and lever arm to fit your body.

Lever Seated Leg Press

Adjust seat so feet rest flat with knees at 90 degrees. Place feet shoulder-width on footplate. Grip handles, push platform away by extending legs fully, then lower with control.

3-4 sets x 10-12 reps

Targets: Quadriceps, Glutes, Hamstrings, Calves

Tip: Low-impact option for those with joint pain. Controlled movement with adjustable resistance.

Sled Hack Squat

Stand on platform with feet shoulder-width, toes forward. Grasp handles, keep back straight and chest up. Lower by bending knees until thighs are parallel, then drive through heels to return.

3-4 sets x 8-12 reps

Targets: Quadriceps, Glutes, Hamstrings, Calves

Tip: Push through heels and maintain control during descent. Great quad builder with back support.

Calf Exercises Home-Friendly

Don't neglect your calves! Build strong lower legs with these exercises.

Dumbbell Calf Raise

Hold dumbbells at sides. Stand on edge of step with heels hanging off. Rise up onto toes as high as possible, then lower heels below step level for full stretch.

3-4 sets x 15-20 reps

Targets: Calves (Gastrocnemius)

Single Leg Calf Raise

Same as regular calf raise but on one leg. Hold dumbbell in one hand, other hand on wall for balance. More challenging and ensures balanced development.

3 sets x 12-15 each leg

Targets: Calves, Balance

Seated Calf Raise

Sit on bench with dumbbells on knees. Place balls of feet on platform or step. Raise heels up, then lower below platform level. Targets soleus muscle.

3-4 sets x 15-20 reps

Targets: Calves (Soleus)

Tip: Do both standing (gastrocnemius) and seated (soleus) calf raises for complete calf development.

Home Leg Training Tips

  • Start with bodyweight exercises to master form before adding weight
  • Dumbbells are highly versatile for home leg training - invest in adjustable ones
  • A stability ball and resistance bands greatly expand exercise options
  • For glute ham raises, secure ankles under couch or have partner hold them
  • Progressive overload: increase reps, then sets, then weight as you get stronger
  • Single leg exercises are excellent for home training - very effective with light weights
  • Don't skip hamstrings! They're crucial for knee health and injury prevention
  • Include both hip hinge exercises (RDLs, good mornings) and knee flexion (curls, glute bridges)

Cardio Exercises

High-Intensity Cardio Full Body

Cardiovascular exercises that build endurance, burn calories, and improve overall conditioning.

Burpee With Push-up Advanced

A full-body exercise combining a squat, plank, push-up, and jump. Start standing, drop to plank, perform a push-up, jump feet forward, then explode upward with arms overhead.

3 sets x 10-15 reps Bodyweight Details

Targets: Chest, Shoulders, Triceps, Core, Glutes, Quads, Hamstrings, Calves

How to: 1) Stand feet shoulder-width apart 2) Squat, hands on ground 3) Jump feet back to plank 4) Perform push-up 5) Jump feet to hands 6) Explode upward with jump

Jump Step Up Intermediate

A plyometric exercise involving jumping onto a raised platform or step, then stepping back down. Builds explosive lower body power and cardiovascular endurance.

3 sets x 10-12 reps each leg Bodyweight Details

Targets: Quadriceps, Glutes, Hamstrings, Calves, Core

How to: 1) Stand facing sturdy box/step at knee height 2) Place foot on box, push through heel 3) Drive up explosively, bring other foot up 4) Step back down 5) Alternate lead leg each rep

Mountain Climber Beginner

A high-intensity cardio and core exercise performed in plank position. Rapidly alternate driving knees toward chest while maintaining a stable upper body.

3 sets x 30 seconds Bodyweight Details

Targets: Core, Obliques, Quads, Hip Flexors, Shoulders, Chest

How to: 1) Start in plank, hands under shoulders 2) Keep body in straight line 3) Drive right knee to chest 4) Rapidly switch legs 5) Maintain core tight throughout

Cardio Training Tips

  • Start with fewer reps and focus on proper form before increasing intensity
  • Rest 30-60 seconds between sets for conditioning, longer for strength focus
  • Burpees can be modified - skip the push-up or jump for lower intensity
  • Combine with other exercises for HIIT circuits
  • Keep core engaged throughout to protect lower back

Wrist Training

Wrist & Forearm Strength Grip + Stability

Build grip strength, wrist stability, and forearm endurance. These exercises improve your performance on every pull, curl, and carry while protecting against wrist injuries.

Kettlebell Punch Intermediate

Hold a kettlebell by the handle in one hand. Extend your arm forward in a punching motion while gripping tightly, then retract. Builds wrist stability and grip endurance.

3 sets x 10-12 each hand Video

Targets: Wrist Flexors, Forearm Extensors, Grip

Towel Hand to Hand Curl Intermediate

Thread a towel through a kettlebell handle. Grip each end of the towel and curl up, transferring the kettlebell from one hand to the other at the top. The towel grip adds forearm challenge.

3 sets x 10-12 reps Video

Targets: Forearms, Wrist Flexors, Biceps, Grip

Reverse Curl Beginner

Hold dumbbells or barbell with an overhand (pronated) grip. Curl the weight up while keeping palms facing down throughout. Targets the brachioradialis and wrist extensors.

3 sets x 12-15 reps Video

Targets: Brachioradialis, Wrist Extensors, Forearms

Towel Kettlebell Curl Intermediate

Thread a towel through a kettlebell handle and grip both ends of the towel together. Perform bicep curls while the towel forces your grip and wrist stabilizers to work overtime.

3 sets x 10-12 reps Video

Targets: Forearms, Wrist Stabilizers, Biceps, Grip

Kettlebell Wrist Curl Beginner

Sit on a bench with forearm resting on thigh, palm facing up, holding a kettlebell. Lower the kettlebell by extending the wrist, then curl it back up using only wrist movement.

3 sets x 12-15 each hand Video

Targets: Wrist Flexors, Forearm Flexors, Grip

Wrist Training Tips

  • Start with light weight and focus on controlled, full range-of-motion reps to avoid strain
  • Warm up your wrists with gentle circles and flexion/extension stretches before training
  • Towel exercises dramatically increase grip difficulty - reduce weight by 30-40% compared to normal grip
  • Train wrists at the end of your session so grip fatigue does not compromise other lifts
  • If you feel sharp pain (not muscle burn), stop immediately - wrist joints are delicate
  • Pair wrist curls with reverse curls to maintain balanced forearm development
